# Confidant Test Queries

Sample prompts for testing the desktop app Chat interface. Use these in the Chat tab to explore the agent's capabilities.

For mental health topics, try prompts like: "I'm feeling stressed," "How can gratitude help my mood?," or "What are signs I should talk to someone?"

---

## 1. Logical Reasoning

### Basic Logic
- "If all cats are mammals, and Fluffy is a cat, what can we conclude about Fluffy?"
- "If it's raining, then the ground is wet. The ground is not wet. What can we conclude?"
- "Every bird can fly. A penguin is a bird. Can a penguin fly? Explain the contradiction."

### Conditional Logic
- "If I study hard, I will pass the exam. I did not study hard. Will I pass?"
- "Either it's sunny or it's raining. It's not sunny. What is the weather?"

### Deductive Reasoning
- "All roses are flowers. All flowers need water. Do roses need water?"
- "No reptiles are warm-blooded. All snakes are reptiles. Are snakes warm-blooded?"

## 2. Mathematical Reasoning

### Basic Arithmetic
- "What is 15 multiplied by 23?"
- "If I have 47 apples and give away 19, how many do I have left?"
- "Calculate: (12 + 8) Ã— 3 - 15"

### Word Problems
- "Sarah has 3 times as many books as Tom. Tom has 5 books. How many books does Sarah have?"
- "A train travels 120 miles in 2 hours. How fast is it going in miles per hour?"
- "If a pizza is cut into 8 slices and 3 people each eat 2 slices, how many slices are left?"

### Pattern Recognition
- "What comes next in this sequence: 2, 4, 8, 16, ?"
- "Complete the pattern: 5, 10, 20, 40, ?"
- "What is the 10th number in the sequence: 1, 4, 9, 16, 25...?"

### Fractions and Percentages
- "What is 25% of 80?"
- "If 3/4 of a class of 24 students passed, how many students passed?"
- "A shirt costs $40. It's on sale for 20% off. What's the sale price?"

---

## 3. Causal Reasoning

### Cause and Effect
- "Why do plants need sunlight?"
- "What happens to water when it reaches 100 degrees Celsius?"
- "If you don't water a plant, what will happen and why?"

### Scientific Reasoning
- "Why does ice float on water?"
- "What causes the seasons to change?"
- "Explain why we see lightning before we hear thunder."

### Chain of Events
- "Describe what happens when you plant a seed: what are the steps from seed to plant?"
- "What happens to food after you eat it? Trace the process."

---

## 4. Comparative Reasoning

### Comparisons
- "What's the difference between a lake and an ocean?"
- "Compare and contrast cats and dogs as pets."
- "How is a bicycle different from a motorcycle?"

### Analogies
- "A book is to reading as a movie is to what?"
- "If a hammer is to a nail, what is a screwdriver to?"
- "Complete the analogy: Bird is to nest as bee is to ?"

### Ranking and Ordering
- "Arrange these in order from smallest to largest: planet, star, galaxy, moon"
- "Which is heavier: a kilogram of feathers or a kilogram of steel? Explain."

---

## 5. Problem-Solving

### Practical Problems
- "I need to bake a cake that takes 45 minutes, but I only have a timer that goes up to 30 minutes. How can I solve this?"
- "You have 3 liters of water and need to measure exactly 1 liter using only a 5-liter and 2-liter container. How do you do it?"
- "A farmer has 17 sheep. All but 9 die. How many are left?"

### Optimization
- "If you can only carry 10 items and need to prioritize: food, water, shelter, tools, and entertainment, what would you choose and why?"
- "You have $100 to spend. A book costs $15, a movie ticket costs $12, and a meal costs $20. What's the maximum number of each you can buy?"

---

## 6. Multi-Step Reasoning

### Complex Problems
- "If a store has 50 apples, sells 15 on Monday, receives 20 more on Tuesday, and sells 10 on Wednesday, how many apples does it have left?"
- "A recipe calls for 2 cups of flour to make 12 cookies. How many cups of flour do you need to make 36 cookies?"
- "You're planning a trip: 3 hours to drive to the airport, 2 hours before flight, 5 hours flight time, and 1 hour to get to your hotel. How long is your total travel time?"

### Sequential Logic
- "First, I wake up. Then I brush my teeth. After that, I eat breakfast. What do I do before eating breakfast?"
- "If step 1 is 'prepare ingredients', step 2 is 'mix them', and step 3 is 'bake', what happens between steps 2 and 3?"

---

## 7. Abstract Reasoning

### Concepts and Categories
- "What do a car, a bicycle, and a skateboard have in common?"
- "Which doesn't belong: apple, orange, banana, carrot? Why?"
- "What is the category that includes: whale, dolphin, and shark?"

### Definitions
- "What makes something a 'tool'? Give examples."
- "Define what it means for something to be 'alive'."
- "What is the difference between 'weather' and 'climate'?"

---

## 8. Common Sense Reasoning

### Everyday Knowledge
- "Why shouldn't you touch a hot stove?"
- "What would happen if you tried to breathe underwater without equipment?"
- "Why do we wear warm clothes in winter?"

### Social Reasoning
- "If someone is crying, what might be wrong and how could you help?"
- "Why is it important to say 'please' and 'thank you'?"
- "What should you do if you see someone drop their wallet?"

## 9. Knowledge Bank Integration Tests

### Wikipedia Knowledge
- "What is Antarctica?"
- "Tell me about the .aq domain"
- "What do you know about science from your knowledge base?"

### RAG Testing
- "Based on your knowledge bank, what can you tell me about [topic from Wikipedia articles]?"
- "Search your knowledge for information about [subject]"
- "What information do you have stored about [topic]?"

## 10. Creative and Hypothetical Reasoning

### "What If" Questions
- "What would happen if gravity was twice as strong on Earth?"
- "If you could only use one sense for a day, which would you choose and why?"
- "What if plants could talk? How would that change the world?"

### Creative Problem-Solving
- "How would you get a ping pong ball out of a 10-foot deep hole using only a rope, a bucket, and a magnet?"
- "Design a way to keep ice from melting without a refrigerator."
- "How could you measure the height of a tall building using only a barometer?"

---

## 11. Temporal Reasoning

### Time-Based Logic
- "If today is Monday and an event is in 5 days, what day is the event?"
- "If breakfast is at 8 AM and lunch is 4 hours later, what time is lunch?"
- "A movie starts at 7:30 PM and lasts 2 hours 15 minutes. What time does it end?"

### Before/After Logic
- "What comes before Wednesday?"
- "If spring comes after winter, what comes after spring?"

---

## 12. Spatial Reasoning

### Directions and Positions
- "If I'm facing north and turn right, what direction am I facing?"
- "If a book is on a shelf above a table, and the table is on the floor, where is the book relative to the floor?"
- "Describe the relative positions: The cat is under the table. The table is in the kitchen. Where is the cat?"

---

## Testing Tips

1. **Start Simple**: Begin with basic logical and mathematical queries
2. **Progress Gradually**: Move to multi-step and abstract reasoning
3. **Test RAG**: Verify the knowledge bank is being used appropriately
4. **Note Patterns**: Observe where the agent struggles or excels
5. **Compare Responses**: Test the same query multiple times to check consistency
6. **Context Testing**: Try follow-up questions to test conversation memory

---

## Expected Behaviors

- **Good Reasoning**: Clear logical steps, correct conclusions, explanations
- **RAG Usage**: References to knowledge bank when relevant
- **Conversation Memory**: Remembers context from previous exchanges
- **Honest Uncertainty**: Admits when it doesn't know or is uncertain
- **Appropriate Responses**: Matches response length and detail to query complexity
